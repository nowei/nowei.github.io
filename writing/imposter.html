Imposter syndrome

Imposter syndrome is the feeling of being an imposter.

    It's when you feel like you aren't good enough to be where you are or that someday, someone will realize that you don't belong there. A lot of people have imposter syndrome, but it's prevalent in STEM-related fields.

    It might manifest itself in many ways, you might feel (maybe know) that people around you are better than you and that they have their lives in order; while you're just trying to figure out what you want to do with your life. It might feel like everyone around you is successful and that they're more hard-working, talented, or smarter than you are. It might make you feel small, standing among these giants.

    They might feel the same way.

"I wish I was smart/talented/had a better work ethic."

    No one is born knowing how to walk. (Well, with the exception of migratory animals like giraffes.) We start our lives with little to no knowledge and we spend the rest of our lives learning things.

    No one is born with an underlying knowledge of how the world works. We learn all these things from our interactions with other people and from our own observations. Similarly, no one is born knowing Java or Python, how to talk, how to do maths, cook, etc. We learn these things by putting time and effort into it.

    The internet has become a great equalizer for access to resources that can help you learn what you want to learn. (Inequalities still exist, but I'll touch upon those at another time.) We can only learn something if we spend time learning it and we can only improve at something if we spend time doing it.

Everyone who has learned something started somewhere, but everyone who has learned it has started. No one can expect to become a chess grandmaster without ever playing a game. The same goes for whatever you're trying to do.
